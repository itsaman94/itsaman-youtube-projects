## ❓ Question 1 — The big idea

**In the pizza story, why was *"Give me some food"* a bad order?**

- **A.** The pizza shop was closed
- **B.** The person had to *guess* what you wanted, so you might get anything
- **C.** Food is too expensive
- **D.** You have to order online only

<details>
<summary>✅ <b>Click to reveal the answer</b></summary>

**Answer: B** — Because it's vague, the other person has to **guess**. A clear
order ("one large pepperoni pizza, thin crust...") gets you *exactly* what you
want. Talking to an AI works the same way!

</details>

## ❓ Question 2 — What is a prompt?

**What exactly is an AI "prompt"?**

- **A.** A special secret code only experts know
- **B.** A type of computer programming language
- **C.** The message you type to the AI
- **D.** A paid feature inside ChatGPT

<details>
<summary>✅ <b>Click to reveal the answer</b></summary>

**Answer: C** — A prompt is simply **the message you type to the AI**. If you
write *"Write me a birthday poem for my mom,"* that whole sentence is your prompt.
No code or math required!

</details>

## ❓ Question 3 — The golden rule

**What is the most important thing to remember about how AI works?**

- **A.** The AI can read your mind and knows what you mean
- **B.** The AI only knows what you tell it — it can't guess missing details
- **C.** The AI always gives the perfect answer the first time
- **D.** The AI remembers everything about your whole life

<details>
<summary>✅ <b>Click to reveal the answer</b></summary>

**Answer: B** — The AI is **smart but takes you literally**. Like a brilliant new
intern on day one, it only knows what you actually tell it. The clearer you are,
the better it answers.

</details>

## ❓ Question 4 — The simple recipe

**Which set of four questions makes up the easy "recipe" for a great prompt?**

- **A.** When? Where? Why? How much?
- **B.** Who? What? How? What does it need to know?
- **C.** Buy? Sell? Trade? Hold?
- **D.** Faster? Cheaper? Bigger? Better?

<details>
<summary>✅ <b>Click to reveal the answer</b></summary>

**Answer: B** — The recipe is **Who** (role), **What** (task), **How** (format),
and **What does it need to know** (context). Example: *"You are a friendly fitness
coach. Make me a workout plan. Keep it to 5 short steps. I'm a beginner with 20
minutes a day."*

</details>

## ❓ Question 5 — Lazy vs. great prompts

**Which of these is the BEST (most effective) prompt?**

- **A.** *"Write about coffee."*
- **B.** *"Coffee?"*
- **C.** *"Tell me something."*
- **D.** *"Write a fun 3-sentence Instagram caption about morning coffee, aimed at students."*

<details>
<summary>✅ <b>Click to reveal the answer</b></summary>

**Answer: D** — It tells the AI the **format** (3-sentence caption), the **style**
(fun), the **topic** (morning coffee), and the **audience** (students). The more
detail you give, the more the answer feels made *just for you*.

</details>
